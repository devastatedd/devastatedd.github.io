Animated and designed video graphics for IGN's rebooted Up at Noon, a weekly pop culture variety show hosted by Brian Altano and Max Scoville.


Watch an episode below:


Intro

Once the logo was finalized by our senior designer (Justin Vachon), I was given direction to make an intro that was 90s-Nickolodeon-meets-modern inspired with Double Dare and slime being the main influence. I used the paint splatter in the logo as my main source of inspiration for choosing background colors and shapes, opening animations, and logo animations. Frame by frame paint splatter animation by Jack Taylor.

Transitions

Early transition drafts focused on the paint splatters covering the screen, but it was narrowed down to multicolored triangles from the logo and a wipe over the abstract memphis background from the intro.

Lower Thirds, Upper Thirds, and Namebars

Customizable lower and upper thirds were needed for easy use by the Video Team. This involved me animating it in After Effects and then preparing the lower thirds as time responsive .MOGRT templates for Premiere Pro use.




Multiple name bars were created for use with up to 4 hosts. These namebars were templatized so that the video editor(s) could toggle on and off up to 4 namebars.

Snipe

Designed the snipe for future sponsored segments where a sponsor would need to be shown.


Title Cards

Title Card for episode/segment titles. Template can be toggled from one, two, or three lines with After Effects-to-Premiere .mogrt files.

End Cards

End card for the sizzle and an end card for the regular episodes.
